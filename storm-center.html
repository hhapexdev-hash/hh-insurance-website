Tool 03

Claims: the first 48 hours

What to do — and what to never sign — when the storm has passed.

  • 1 · Safety, then photos.Don't re-enter a flooded structure until power is confirmed off. Then photograph everything before you touch anything.
  • 2 · Stop further damage, keep receipts.Tarps, water extraction, board-up — reasonable mitigation is reimbursable. Document it.
  • 3 · Call your carrier's claim line — or call us first.We'll tell you whether the damage clears your hurricane deductible before you open a claim. Production: full carrier hotline list here.
  • 4 · Don't sign an AOB on the driveway.Assignment-of-benefits contracts from door-knockers can sign away control of your claim. Have us read anything first — it's free.
  • 5 · Keep a claim diary.Every call, name, and promise, with dates. It's boring and it wins disputes.
